## 🛠 What is gorisk?

gorisk is a tool that helps you see and understand the risks in the software you use. It maps what your software’s parts (called dependencies) can do. This helps you learn if those parts might cause security problems or other risks. gorisk checks your software supply chain, meaning it looks at all the smaller pieces that make your software work.

You do not need to know programming to use gorisk. It works on Windows and runs from your computer.

## ⚙️ Using gorisk: A Simple Guide

Once gorisk is open, it lets you check your software for supply-chain risks. Here is a simple way to get started:

1. **Select or enter your project details.** gorisk will ask where your project is or what dependencies you want to check.
2. **Scan your dependencies.** Click the "Start Scan" button. gorisk will analyze all the parts your project uses.
3. **Review the report.** After the scan, gorisk shows you what it found. It lists known risks or vulnerabilities.
4. **Take action.** If gorisk finds risky parts, you can decide to update or change those parts to keep your software safe.

## 🔍 What gorisk Looks For

gorisk focuses on the following areas when checking your software:

- **Security issues (CVEs).** It checks databases for known vulnerabilities in your dependencies.
- **Dependency list.** It identifies all the smaller pieces of code used.
- **Risk mapping.** It shows what the parts can do and any risks they might carry.
- **Supply-chain problems.** It spots weak points in how your software is built from other software parts.
